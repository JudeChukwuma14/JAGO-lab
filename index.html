Hepatitis B

The Hepatitis B test is utilized to detect the presence of the Hepatitis B virus (HBV) in the bloodstream. It involves analyzing a blood sample to identify specific viral markers, such as antigens and antibodies, which help determine if a person has been exposed to the virus or is currently infected.

HIV/AIDS

The HIV/AIDS test is employed to diagnose the Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV), which leads to Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS). Early detection of HIV is essential for initiating appropriate medical treatment, managing the disease's progression, and adopting preventive measures to protect others from infection.

MP/WIDAL

The MP and Widal tests are commonly conducted together. The MP test identifies the presence of the malaria parasite in the blood, aiding in malaria diagnosis. The Widal test, on the other hand, helps diagnose enteric fever. These tests are crucial for the timely identification and treatment of malaria and enteric fever.

PREGNANCY TEST

The Pregnancy test is widely used to determine if a woman is pregnant. The test can confirm pregnancy within a few days after a missed menstrual period. Early detection of pregnancy allows expectant mothers to initiate prenatal care and make informed decisions regarding their health.
